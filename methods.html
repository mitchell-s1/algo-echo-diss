Research Methods

Design Overview

Four mixed-methods studies test whether algorithmic feeds trigger regressive restoration of the self via deprivation curiosity, moralization, and identity fusion.

Study 1 – Curiosity & Misinformation Uptake (N = 300)

  • IV: Deprivation Curiosity × Ambiguity (high/low)
  • DV: Belief endorsement for novel false headlines
  • Analysis: Hierarchical regression, preregistered on OSF

Study 2 – Moral Framing & Resistance to Correction (N = 240)

  • 2 × 2: moral vs neutral framing × identity relevance
  • DV: belief persistence post-correction
  • Mediation: identity threat (PROCESS Model 8)

Study 3 – EMA Smartphone Protocol (N = 180, 14 days)

  • Manipulate personalised vs diverse headlines via Slack bot
  • DV: daily certainty & paranoia (GPTS-5)
  • Analysis: multilevel model (days nested in persons)

Study 4 – Micro-RCT of ACT Intervention (N = 120)

  • Three arms: ACT defusion vs literacy vs wait-list
  • Primary outcomes: belief flexibility & identity fusion

All materials and data will be released on the OSF project page.
