Project Overview

Algorithmic feeds—optimized for engagement, not truth—create chronic epistemic instability. My dissertation integrates depth-psychology, cognitive science and clinical theory to test whether these environments trigger regressive restoration of the self: a retreat to rigid, absolutist meaning structures that regulate distress at the cost of flexibility and reality-testing.

Cognitive

Deprivation curiosity & illusory-truth loops.

Moral

Identity-protective cognition & moralization.

Clinical

Amplification of schizotypy, paranoia, thought disorder.

Integrated Ecological Model

Algorithmic reinforcement → Epistemic destabilization → Identity fusion / symptom amplification

Empirical Studies

  1. Study 1 – Curiosity & Misinformation Uptake
    Cross-sectional survey (N = 300): Does deprivation curiosity predict belief in ambiguous false headlines after controlling for CRT and need for closure?
  2. Study 2 – Moral Framing & Resistance to Correction
    2×2 experiment (N = 240): Moral vs neutral framing × high vs low identity relevance → belief persistence post-correction.
  3. Study 3 – Ecological Momentary Assessment
    14-day smartphone protocol (N = 180): Personalised vs diverse feed simulation → daily certainty & paranoia trajectories.
  4. Study 4 – Micro-RCT of ACT Intervention
    3-arm trial (N = 120): 45-min ACT defusion vs literacy vs wait-list → cognitive flexibility & identity fusion.
